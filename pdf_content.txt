7.4  DPLE  Routing  Logic  (How  Routes  
Are
 
Optimized)
 
DPLE  optimizes  6  variables:  
1.  Distance  
2.  Load  weight  
3.  Vehicle  type  
4.  Road  &  traffic  conditions  
5.  Pickup/drop  sequence  
6.  Time  sensitivity  of  crop   
Routing  Algorithm  Steps  
1.  Cluster  nearby  farmers  (<3–5  km  radius)  
 2.  Calculate  minimum  billable  weight  (70%  truck  capacity)  
 3.  Assign  optimal  vehicle  (Auto/Tempo/Mini  Truck/Cold  Chain)  
 4.  Apply  deadhead  adjustment  (+10%  km)  
 5.  Compute  cost  per  kg  
 6.  Add  Risk  Buffer  (2–4%)  
 7.  Finalize  buyer’s  delivered  price  
 
 
Example:  Multi-Pickup  Route  Farmer  A  →  Farmer  B  →  Farmer  C        ↓         ↓          ↓  Buyer  1  →  Buyer  2  →  Buyer  3   
One  tempo  replaces  3  separate  trips:  
●  Saves  fuel  
 ●  Reduces  costs  
 
●  Increases  hauler  earnings  
 ●  Decreases  buyer  landed  price  
 
This  is  the  foundation  of  our  profit  margin  moat .

 2.  Perform  10%  crate  shuffle  
 3.  Take  3  verification  images  
 4.  Select  grade  
 5.  Upload  geo-stamped  photos  
 6.  Apply  Digital  Twin  QR  code  
 7.  Approve  listing  
 
 
SOP  2:  Hauler  Dispatch  Workflow  
1.  Hauler  receives  job  request  
 2.  Accept  →  Route  visible  
 3.  Scan  crate  QR  
 4.  Begin  trip  
 5.  Complete  multi-pickup/drops  
 6.  Farmer  confirms  pickup  
 7.  Buyer  confirms  delivery  
 8.  UPI  payout  released  instantly  
 
 
SOP  3:  Buyer  Dispute  Submission  
1.  Scan  crate  QR  
 2.  Upload  defect  video  
 3.  Submit  within  2  hours  
 4.  AI  compares  before/after  
 5.  Admin  decides  
 6.  Refund  auto-credited
